# Cyber Summit 2026 — Event Guide

## Overview

Cyber Summit 2026 is a hands-on cybersecurity conference designed for high school students across Loudoun County who are interested in technology, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and ethical hacking.

The event brings together students, mentors, and industry professionals for a full day of interactive workshops, technical challenges, and collaborative learning.

The goal is to inspire the next generation of cybersecurity professionals by giving students real-world exposure to tools, techniques, and career paths within the field.

## Event Details

- **Event:** Cyber Summit 2026
- **Date:** Saturday, May 30, 2026
- **Time:** 9:00 AM–4:00 PM
- **Location:** Briar Woods High School
- **Address:** 22525 Belmont Ridge Rd, Ashburn, VA 20148

## Who Should Attend

- High school students interested in cybersecurity, AI, and technology
- Beginners exploring cybersecurity for the first time
- Students involved in CyberPatriot, coding clubs, or tech programs
- Anyone curious about ethical hacking and digital defense

**No prior cybersecurity experience is required for most activities.**

## What Students Will Experience

Students will participate in hands-on cybersecurity activities designed to simulate real-world scenarios. These challenges encourage teamwork, critical thinking, and technical exploration.

Participants will gain experience with:

- Cybersecurity tools
- Digital investigation techniques
- Ethical hacking concepts
- Artificial intelligence security
- Hardware security and IoT devices

Each workshop is guided by mentors and student leaders.

## Activities and Workshops

### Cyber Escape Room
Students work in teams to solve cybersecurity puzzles and digital clues in order to “break out” of a simulated cyber incident. Challenges include password cracking, log analysis, cryptography puzzles, and system forensics.

**Skills learned:** problem solving, teamwork, digital investigation

### AI Capture The Flag (AI CTF)
Students compete in cybersecurity challenges involving artificial intelligence systems. Participants analyze AI models, identify vulnerabilities, and attempt to exploit or defend AI-driven systems.

**Topics may include:** prompt injection attacks, AI model manipulation, adversarial AI techniques, AI security concepts

### AI Hardware Lab
This workshop introduces students to building and setting up an Arduino and training a simple AI model that can recognize human emotions. Participants connect their AI model to physical hardware, creating a system where facial expressions control lights or a small display in real time.

**Topics include:** emotion-recognition AI models, connecting AI outputs to microcontrollers, real-time hardware reactions, and fundamentals of AI-driven embedded systems

### IoT Penetration Testing Lab
Students explore the security of Internet of Things (IoT) devices such as smart cameras, sensors, and embedded systems.

Participants will:

- Analyze IoT devices
- Identify vulnerabilities
- Perform beginner penetration testing
- Learn how IoT devices are secured in the real world
